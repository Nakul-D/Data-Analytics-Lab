World War II, lasting from 1939 to 1945, was a global conflict involving many of the world's nations.
The war began when Nazi Germany, led by Adolf Hitler, invaded Poland.
The Axis Powers, including Germany, Italy, and Japan, fought against the Allied Powers,
primarily the United States, the Soviet Union, the United Kingdom, and China.
The war led to significant battles such as D-Day and the Pacific theater's island-hopping.
The Holocaust, a genocide carried out by Germany, resulted in the death of six million Jews.
The war ended with the unconditional surrender of Germany and Japan in 1945.
